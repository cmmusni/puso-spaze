# Product Context — PUSO Spaze

## Why This Project Exists
Mental health support in the Philippines, especially for Gen Z, lacks accessible and stigma-free platforms. PUSO Spaze ("puso" means "heart" in Filipino) creates a safe space where users can express themselves anonymously and receive compassion from peers and trained coaches, reinforced with faith-based encouragement.

## Problems It Solves
- **Stigma barrier** — Anonymous posting removes fear of judgment
- **Lack of accessible support** — Available on web and mobile, no account email required
- **Content safety** — AI moderation + coach review prevents harmful content
- **Isolation** — Community reactions and comments provide human connection
- **Spiritual support gap** — AI-generated biblical encouragement in Taglish (Tagalog + English) resonates with the audience

## How It Should Work
1. **Users** enter with a username (custom or anonymous-generated), no password needed — device-bound identity
2. **Post** thoughts, struggles, prayers — optionally anonymous with tags (#struggling, #prayer, #gratitude, #encouragement)
3. **Community** reacts and comments; coaches moderate flagged content
4. **Journal** privately to track mood and reflections over time
5. **Chat** 1:1 with coaches for deeper support
6. **Hourly Hope** — automated encouraging posts appear periodically

## User Experience Goals
- **Warmth & safety** — "Sacred Journal" design makes the app feel like a personal safe space
- **Zero friction** — No sign-up forms, email, or passwords; just pick a name and start
- **Mobile-first, web-ready** — Full feature parity across platforms
- **Responsive** — Graceful adaptation from phone to tablet to wide desktop
- **Coach empowerment** — Dashboard gives coaches efficient moderation tools + community sentiment overview
