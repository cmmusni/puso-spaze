Child Safety Standards

Our commitment to preventing, detecting, and reporting child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) on PUSO Spaze.

Effective Date: April 22, 2026

1. Our Commitment

PUSO Spaze is a mental wellness community app. The safety of children and young people on our platform is non-negotiable. We adhere to a zero-tolerance policy against child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), including the creation, sharing, solicitation, or promotion of child sexual abuse material (CSAM).

This page outlines the standards, controls, and reporting channels we maintain to keep PUSO Spaze a safe space for every user.

2. Age Requirement

  • PUSO Spaze is intended for users aged 13 and older.
  • Users under 13 are not permitted to create accounts or use the service.
  • If we become aware that a user under 13 has created an account, we will permanently delete the account and all associated data.

3. Prohibited Content & Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on PUSO Spaze and will result in immediate account removal and reporting to the relevant authorities:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any form — text, image, video, or link.
  • Sexualized depictions, descriptions, or discussions of minors.
  • Grooming behavior — building emotional connection with a minor for sexual purposes.
  • Solicitation or trading of CSAM.
  • Sextortion, sexual harassment, or coercion targeting minors.
  • Sharing of personally identifying information of minors without consent.
  • Any content that endangers, exploits, or harms a child.

4. How We Prevent & Detect Abuse

4.1 AI-Powered Moderation

Every post and comment submitted to PUSO Spaze is automatically scanned before publication using a combination of:

  • Local keyword filters for high-risk terms.
  • OpenAI Moderation API to detect sexual content involving minors, harassment, and other policy violations.
  • Default-deny posture — when moderation is uncertain, content is held for human review rather than auto-published.

4.2 Image Validation

  • Uploaded images are validated for type and size on both client and server.
  • All images are stored on Cloudinary with content moderation hooks available.

4.3 Human Review

  • Trained coaches and admins review flagged posts, comments, and reports.
  • Coaches have the ability to remove content, suspend users, and escalate cases.

5. In-App Reporting

Every user can report content or behavior that violates our standards directly inside the app:

  • Tap the ⋯ (more) menu on any post or comment and select Report.
  • Reports are sent to our coach and admin team for immediate review.
  • Users can also block other users to prevent further interaction.
  • Reports involving suspected CSAE are prioritized and acted on with urgency.

6. Reporting CSAE to Authorities

When CSAE is identified on PUSO Spaze, we:

  • Immediately remove the offending content.
  • Suspend or permanently terminate the responsible account(s).
  • Preserve relevant records for law enforcement.
  • Report to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) via the CyberTipline at report.cybertip.org, and to other regional authorities as required by law.

7. Reporting CSAE to PUSO Spaze

If you witness or suspect CSAE on PUSO Spaze, please report it immediately:

Email: cliffordmarkmusni@gmail.com

In-app: Use the Report option on the post or comment.

For emergencies involving an imminent threat to a child, contact your local emergency services first.

8. Designated Child Safety Contact

Our designated point of contact for child safety matters is reachable at cliffordmarkmusni@gmail.com. We respond to verified reports as quickly as possible.

9. Compliance With Applicable Laws

PUSO Spaze complies with all applicable child safety laws in the jurisdictions where we operate, including but not limited to:

  • U.S. federal laws on the reporting of CSAM (18 U.S.C. § 2258A).
  • The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).
  • Relevant child protection laws in the Philippines and other regions.
  • Google Play's Child Safety Standards Policy.

10. Continuous Improvement

We regularly review and update our moderation tooling, reporting flows, and coach training to strengthen child safety on the platform. This page is updated when our standards change.

11. Contact

For questions about these Child Safety Standards, contact us at cliffordmarkmusni@gmail.com.
